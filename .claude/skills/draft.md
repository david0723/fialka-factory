# Draft Skill

## Purpose

Write blog post drafts for "unpacked by fialka." Every piece must sound authentically like Kaja.

**CRITICAL:** Read `.claude/skills/writing-style.md` before writing ANY content. Also read 1-2 articles in `reference/` to internalize her rhythm.

## Process

1. **Gather inputs**
   - Read all `research/YYYY-MM-DD.md` files from the past 14 days
   - Read `reference/` articles to calibrate voice
   - Check previous drafts in `drafts/` to avoid repeating topics

2. **Determine article type**
   - Read the issue body for guidance on type
   - Default to Opinion/Critical Essay if unspecified
   - Types: Opinion Essay (~3000 words), Personal Narrative (~2500-4000 words), Niche Bucketlist (~1800-2000 words)

3. **Write the draft**
   - Follow the structure for the chosen type (see writing-style.md)
   - Write in fialka's voice: conversational, sardonic, self-implicating, nuanced
   - **Lowercase title always**
   - Open with a hook that makes someone stop scrolling
   - Weave personal experience throughout (draw from reference articles for tone)
   - Build to a nuanced "it depends" conclusion (not black/white)
   - End with something that lingers

4. **Self-review checklist**
   - [ ] Title is lowercase, provocative, and specific
   - [ ] No em dashes (use parentheses, commas, or periods)
   - [ ] No formal/academic tone
   - [ ] No "Top 10" or listicle format (except Niche Bucketlist)
   - [ ] No toxic positivity or influencer language
   - [ ] At least one self-implicating moment (admits own hypocrisy)
   - [ ] At least 3 parenthetical asides for personality
   - [ ] Mix of very short and very long sentences
   - [ ] Starts sentences with "And," "But," "So" at least twice
   - [ ] Specific sensory details (smells, sounds, textures)
   - [ ] Rhetorical questions to engage the reader
   - [ ] Would Kaja actually publish this under her name?
   - [ ] Is there a moment of genuine vulnerability or uncertainty?

## Voice Quick Reference

**Do say:**
- "Look, I get it."
- "And honestly? I'm not sure I have the answer."
- "(or maybe that's just me?)"
- "Here's the thing though."
- "Duh."

**Don't say:**
- "In this article, we will explore..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Travelers should consider..."
- "This revolutionary approach to tourism..."

## Output

Save to `drafts/YYYY-MM-DD-draft.md`.
