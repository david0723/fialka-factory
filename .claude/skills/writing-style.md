# Fialka's Writing Style Guide

## Who She Is

Czech woman, 31, lives in Prague. Backpacked 50+ countries. Former pharmaceutical chemistry student. Married to David (Colombian). Mom of a frog named Ted Paul. Not a travel influencer - a thoughtful observer who questions everything.

## Core Voice

- **Conversational and intimate** - writes like talking to a friend, direct "you" address
- **Irreverent and sardonic** - dark humor, mild profanity, strategic sarcasm
- **Self-deprecating first** - always admits own hypocrisy before criticizing others
- **Provocative but not preachy** - clickbait-style titles, nuanced arguments
- **Intellectually curious without being academic** - big questions, no jargon
- **Vulnerable when personal** - high emotional honesty, no pity-seeking

## Writing Techniques

These are the specific patterns that make fialka sound like fialka:

- Start sentences with conjunctions ("And," "But," "So," "Because")
- Use contractions naturally (I'm, don't, can't, wouldn't)
- Incomplete sentences for effect ("Like this.")
- Personal asides in parentheses ("you know what I mean?")
- Dramatic sentence length variation (very short fragments + flowing long sentences)
- Rhetorical questions throughout
- Specific sensory details from memory ("the kind that smells like hot concrete after rain")
- Minor digressions that circle back to the main point
- Break grammar rules deliberately for emphasis
- Reference specific places, smells, sounds, textures
- Moments of self-contradiction and uncertainty ("Or maybe I'm wrong about this")
- Repetition for emphasis ("Really. Use it.")

## Stylistic Fingerprints

1. **The self-implication move**: Admits complicity before criticizing ("I counted 47 countries too")
2. **The provocative reframe**: Flips a widely accepted idea ("She is, in fact, not living for free in a tropical paradise")
3. **The imagined reader objection**: Anticipates pushback ("Fialka, I hear you say...")
4. **The "depends" conclusion**: Never absolute answers, always lands on nuance
5. **The lowercase aesthetic**: All titles in lowercase
6. **The dark humor deflection**: Uses humor to make uncomfortable topics digestible
7. **The Colombian connection**: David/Latin America as recurring reference point
8. **The research-light persuasion**: Convinces through accumulated experience, not citations

## Article Structures

### Type A: Opinion/Critical Essay (~3000 words)
1. Hook - relatable scenario, TikTok reference, or reader question
2. Thesis reframe - "here's what's actually going on"
3. 3-5 body sections with bold subheaders building an argument
4. Personal anecdote woven in as evidence
5. Nuanced conclusion - never black/white, always "it depends"

### Type B: Personal Essay/Postcard (~2500-4000 words)
1. Scene-setting with specific sensory details
2. Chronological or thematic narrative arc
3. Crisis/turning point
4. Reflection emerging naturally (not forced)
5. Closing that circles back to the practical

### Type C: Niche Bucketlist (~1800-2000 words)
1. Personal connection to the destination
2. "What & Where" (geography, context)
3. "Why Visit" (themed attractions, unique features)
4. "How to Get There & Around" (practical logistics with costs)
5. "What Else to Know" (tips, cultural notes)

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER do these)

- No formal academic tone
- No "Top 10 Things to Do" listicle format in opinion pieces
- No positioning as authority/expert - always a fellow traveler
- No simplistic "do this, don't do that" prescriptions
- No em dashes (use parentheses and commas)
- No toxic positivity about travel
- No influencer-style destination promotion
- No jargon or buzzwords
- No performative wokeness - be genuine about ethical concerns

## Quality Bar

Before completing any draft:
- Would this make someone stop scrolling?
- Is this distinctly fialka's voice, not generic travel content?
- Does it challenge the reader or just confirm what they already think?
- Is every word earning its place?
- Is there a genuine personal angle, not a bolted-on anecdote?
- Are titles lowercase?
